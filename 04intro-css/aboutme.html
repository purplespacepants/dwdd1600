Why I do art

I have to. I can't not. If you aren't doing art I would encourage you take a look inside yourself to find out why. Is it because you're scared of failing? Nice job doofus, you've made yourself miserable for no reason. Art doesn't just belong in a gallery. It belongs in your home. In your heart. In your hands. Art is human, and not just Homo sapiens human. Do you think you're better than art? Than artists? Or are you scared of looking too long in the mirror of your own creations? Don't be scared of yourself. Don't be afraid of being human.

The big art words you should know

For some solid examples of these terms, visit theartteacher.net


Line
You know what a line is, but what's important is what it does. Is it long or short? Curved or jagged? Was it made with a thin pen or a thick brush? Does it vary in thickness? Where does it point? Where does it start and end? These are all important things to think about when assessing the lines in a work of art.
Shape
Shape refers to the two-dimensional aspects of a work. Think circles and squares and triangles. A shape is a space that lines make together, forming a fenced off area. What kinds of shapes do you see?
Form
This is the depth of a work. It can be literal as in the 3d form of a sculpture, or a trick of the eye in the way that an artist can paint something that looks like it's going back in space.
Color
Color is made up of hue, value, and saturation. The hue is the broad answer to the question "what color is that?" Perhaps the answer would just be "green." The value is its lightness or darkness. The saturation is how vibrant the color is.
Tone/Value
This is how light or dark any part of a work is, areas big or small. Controlling tone is what creates the illusion of form.
Texture
That's what you'll feel if you touch the work. Bumpy, smooth, sharp, and things like that. You won't usually be touching an artwork, but if you can see it well then you can usually infer the texture.
Space
Space refers to the stuff that's there and the stuff that isn't. Think of a window. How big is the window? The window divides the work into negative space(the hole that the window creates in the wall) and positive space (the wall itself).

For more terms you can visit the Museum of Modern Art website.
